## 4) Reserve-Specific Factors (Participation, UTA, Active Duty Equivalents)

Reserve promotion competitiveness and eligibility are materially tied to participation quality:
- Satisfactory year/service standards (points-based)
- UTA/IDT participation history
- Unexcused absence history (STEP II explicitly disqualifies recent unexcused absences)
- Currency in readiness elements (e.g., fitness)

From STEP II guide:
- Must have satisfactory participation for prior 12 months
- Any unexcused absences in prior 12 months = not eligible

## 5) PEP / STEP vs Regular Promotion Paths

## Regular Reserve Path (Unit Vacancy style)
- Typically tied to position authorization and vacancy at higher grade.
- Member must meet TIG/TIS/satisfactory service, skill level, PME, and commander recommendation.

## PEP / STEP Path
- STEP II (formerly Promotion Enhancement Program language in older usage) is a commander-driven exception channel to promote exceptional performers.
- Allows promotion **one grade above authorized position grade** under quota controls.

### STEP II mechanics (from AFRC/ARPC guide)
- Annual cycle with PECD (example shown: 30 Sep)
- Promotion effective date often 1 Oct for selects
- TIG/TIS must be met by PECD
- Quotas constrained by:
  - % of enlisted population promoted via STEP II
  - grade manning >100% restriction
- Additional package controls:
  - AF Form 1206
  - fitness proof
  - prior evaluations pulled
  - retainability requirements
  - CCAF requirement at senior levels (as specified)

## 7) Key Differences: Active Duty vs Reserve Enlisted Promotion

1. **Vacancy dependence**
   - **Reserve:** heavily tied to authorized unit position/vacancy (except STEP/PEP-type exception routes).
   - **Active Duty:** enterprise quota-based competition model with broader force-wide promotion opportunity structures.

2. **Participation dimension**
   - **Reserve:** satisfactory participation/points and drill status materially affect promotability.
   - **Active Duty:** continuous full-time service context; no UTA-style participation gate.

3. **Command-driven exception programs**
   - **Reserve:** STEP II/PEP-style mechanisms can promote above position grade with quotas.
   - **Active Duty:** different exception/selection constructs; not primarily vacancy-overgrade at unit level in the same way.

4. **Promotion tempo predictability**
   - **Reserve:** can be slower/more position-dependent.
   - **Active Duty:** more standardized annual cycle competition by grade/AFSC.
