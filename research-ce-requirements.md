## Universal CE Training Framework

All 3E-series AFSCs follow the same CFETP-based progression model:

### Skill Level Progression (All AFSCs)

| Level | Title | How Awarded | Key Requirements |
|-------|-------|-------------|------------------|
| **1-Level** | Helper | Entry into tech school | Enlistment |
| **3-Level** | Apprentice | Graduate tech school | Complete initial skills course |
| **5-Level** | Journeyman | Upgrade after OJT + CDCs | Min 12 months OJT (9 mo for retrainees), complete CDCs, certify all 5-level core & diamond tasks, CE Common Core course |
| **7-Level** | Craftsman | Upgrade (typically at SSgt+) | Complete 7-level craftsman course (if applicable), 7-level OJT tasks, advanced CDCs |
| **9-Level** | Superintendent | SMSgt selection | Experience managing AFSC functions; awarded with SMSgt promotion |

### Common CE Requirements (All 3E AFSCs)

- **CE 5-Level Common Core Concept** — Web-based course; mandatory for all CE 5-level upgrades
- **AFIT WMGT 131** — SMS Builder
- **AFIT WMGT 301** — Intro to Asset Management
- **AFIT WENG 170** — Cybersecurity for Control Systems
- **CCAF Degree** — Desirable (not mandatory for upgrade, but tracked and encouraged; **completion is a promotion board consideration**)
- **Silver Flag** — Required for deployment eligibility (see [CE-Specific Programs](#ce-specific-programs))
- **MICT Checklists** — Continuous compliance; inspectable at any time

### CDC Testing — Promotion-Gating

> ⚠️ **CDCs are promotion-gating.** Airmen who have not completed CDCs are ineligible for promotion testing (WAPS). CDC completion is required before the 5-level can be awarded, and the 5-level is required to test for SSgt.

## 3E0X1 — Electrical Systems

**Mission:** Install, maintain, and repair interior/exterior electrical distribution systems, airfield lighting systems, and associated components.

### Tech School
- **Location:** Sheppard AFB, TX (366th TRS)
- **Length:** ~70 classroom days
- **CCAF:** Mechanical & Electrical Technology (4VGA)

### 5-Level (Journeyman) — AFSC 3E051
- **OJT:** Minimum 12 months (9 months retrainees)
- **CDCs:** Required — **promotion-gating** ⚠️
- **Courses:**
  - CE 5-Level Common Core Concept (web-based)
  - AFIT WMGT 131 (SMS Builder)
  - AFIT WMGT 301 (Intro to Asset Management)
  - AFIT WENG 170 (Cybersecurity for Control Systems)
- **Core Tasks:** All 5^ (5-level core) and ♢ (diamond/equipment-dependent) tasks in the STS must be certified
- **Certifications:** Valid state driver's license required; normal color vision; freedom from fear of heights
- **Desirable Training:** CE Advanced Electrical Troubleshooting, Electrical Distribution System Maintenance, Airfield Lighting Systems (Sheppard AFB)

### 7-Level (Craftsman) — AFSC 3E071
- **Prerequisite:** Must hold AFSC 3E051
- **Mandatory Course:** Electrical Systems Craftsman Course (J3ACR3E071 00AC) — must complete all 7-level OJT requirements *before* attending
- **AFIT WMGT 437:** Troop Construction Project Management Course — **mandatory for AD; required for MSgt promotion** ⚠️
- **Tasks:** Supervision of electrical systems functions, pole climbing, airfield lighting oversight

### 9-Level (Superintendent) — AFSC 3E091
- **Prerequisite:** Hold AFSC 3E071
- **Experience:** Managing electrical systems functions at squadron/group level
- **Awarded:** With promotion to SMSgt

## 3E4X3 — Pest Management

**Mission:** Control pests (insects, rodents, birds, weeds, wildlife) on AF installations. Manage BASH (Bird/Wildlife Aircraft Strike Hazard) programs. Handle and apply pesticides/herbicides in compliance with federal/state/DoD regulations.

### Tech School
- **Location:** Sheppard AFB, TX
- **Length:** ~30 classroom days (6 weeks)
- **CCAF:** Ecological Controls
- **ASVAB:** General (G) ≥ 38
- **Unique Entry Requirement:** Cannot be allergic to bee/spider venom

### 5-Level (Journeyman) — AFSC 3E453
- **OJT:** Minimum 12 months
- **CDCs:** 7 volumes (2 sets) — **promotion-gating** ⚠️
  - Set 1: CE fundamentals, pesticide regulations, general control methods
  - Set 2: Pest-specific control techniques
- **Courses:** CE Common Core, AFIT WMGT 131/301
- **Core Tasks:** Pesticide application, facility inspections, wildlife management, BASH support
- **Certifications:**
  - **DoD Pesticide Applicator Certification** — federally mandated; must maintain current
  - State pesticide applicator certification (where applicable)
  - Normal color vision required (chemical detection)

### 7-Level (Craftsman) — AFSC 3E473
- **Prerequisite:** Hold AFSC 3E453
- **Advanced Course:** Operational Entomology course (Brooks City-Base/current location) — encouraged at SSgt, may become mandatory
- **Recertification:** Week-long recertification class every 3 years at Sheppard AFB — **mandatory** ⚠️
- **AFIT WMGT 437:** Troop Construction Project Management — **required for MSgt promotion** ⚠️

### 9-Level (Superintendent) — AFSC 3E493
- **Awarded:** With SMSgt promotion

### Unique Requirements
- Very small shops (2–6 military per base + 1–2 civilians)
- Pesticide applicator certification must be kept current — **loss of certification affects duty qualification**

## 3E7X1 — Fire Protection

**Mission:** Structural and aircraft rescue firefighting (ARFF), fire prevention, fire inspection, hazardous materials response, and emergency medical response on AF installations.

### Tech School
- **Location:** Goodfellow AFB, TX (Louis F. Garland DoD Fire Academy)
- **Length:** 68 classroom days (~13.5 weeks)
- **CCAF:** Fire Science

### 3-Level Certifications (Earned at Tech School)
All IFSAC and ProBoard accredited — **these are nationally recognized**:
- ✅ **Firefighter I**
- ✅ **Firefighter II**
- ✅ **Hazardous Materials Awareness**
- ✅ **Hazardous Materials Operations**
- ✅ **Airport Firefighter (ARFF)**

### 5-Level (Journeyman) — AFSC 3E751
- **OJT:** Minimum 12 months
- **CDCs:** Required — **promotion-gating** ⚠️
- **Courses:** CE Common Core (fire-specific variant)
- **Core Tasks:** Firefighting operations, ARFF, emergency medical care, CPR, rescue operations
- **Certifications (within first year post-tech school):**
  - ✅ **Driver/Operator — Pumper** (IFSAC/ProBoard)
  - ✅ **Driver/Operator — Mobile Water Supply** (IFSAC/ProBoard)
  - ✅ **Driver/Operator — ARFF** (IFSAC/ProBoard)
- **Other:** Valid driver's license; physical fitness standards (NFPA 1582 medical)

### 7-Level (Craftsman) — AFSC 3E771
- **Prerequisite:** Hold AFSC 3E751
- **Craftsman Course:** Fire Protection Craftsman Course
- **Advanced Certifications Pursued:**
  - ✅ **Fire Officer I** (IFSAC/ProBoard)
  - ✅ **Fire Inspector I** (IFSAC/ProBoard)
  - ✅ **Fire Instructor I** (IFSAC/ProBoard)
  - ✅ **Rescue Technician** (IFSAC/ProBoard)
- **Tasks:** Fire station supervision, fire prevention program management, training oversight

### 9-Level (Superintendent) — AFSC 3E791
- **Prerequisite:** Hold AFSC 3E771
- **Experience:** Managing fire protection functions at installation/group level
- **Awarded:** With SMSgt promotion

### Unique Requirements — Fire Protection
- **DoD F&ES Certification Program (F&ESCP):** Per DoDI 6055.06 and DoD Manual 6055.06-M — all certifications must meet NFPA 1000-series standards
- **IFSAC/ProBoard certifications are career-long** — tracked in DoD F&ESCP system
- **NFPA 1582 Medical:** Annual occupational medical exam required
- **Physical Fitness:** NFPA 1583 fitness standards in addition to standard AF PT test
- **24/48 or 48/96 shift schedules** at most stations (not standard M–F)
- **Certification maintenance:** Some certs require periodic recertification or continuing education hours

> ⚠️ **Fire Protection is unique among CE AFSCs** — it has the most extensive external certification requirements. Loss of medical qualification (NFPA 1582) or failure to maintain certs can affect duty qualification and deployability.

## 3E8X1 — Explosive Ordnance Disposal (EOD)

**Mission:** Detect, identify, render safe, recover, and dispose of explosive ordnance including conventional, nuclear, chemical, biological, and improvised explosive devices (IEDs). Support Secret Service and VIP protective operations.

### Tech School
- **Location:** Naval School Explosive Ordnance Disposal (NAVSCOLEOD), Eglin AFB, FL
- **Length:** ~37 weeks (~180 classroom days) — **longest tech school in CE**
- **CCAF:** Munitions & Weapons Technology
- **ASVAB:** General (G) ≥ 57; Mechanical (M) ≥ 57
- **Security Clearance:** Secret (minimum); Top Secret for some assignments
- **Washout Rate:** High (~50% historically)

### 3-Level (Apprentice) — AFSC 3E831
- Awarded upon graduation from NAVSCOLEOD
- Joint-service training (Navy, Army, Marines, AF together)
- Covers conventional ordnance, IEDs, nuclear, biological, chemical munitions

### 5-Level (Journeyman) — AFSC 3E851
- **OJT:** Minimum 12 months
- **CDCs:** Required — **promotion-gating** ⚠️
- **Core Tasks:** Render safe procedures, IED defeat, range clearance, VIP support operations
- **Certifications:**
  - Hazardous Materials Technician level (typically)
  - Demolition qualifications
  - Various tool/robot certifications (platform-specific)
- **Unique:** Must maintain currency on render-safe procedures through recurring proficiency training

### 7-Level (Craftsman) — AFSC 3E871
- **Prerequisite:** Hold AFSC 3E851
- **Craftsman Course:** EOD Craftsman Course
- **Advanced Courses:** Nuclear, WMD-specific courses; Senior EOD Technician training
- **Tasks:** Team leadership, training program management, incident command

### 9-Level (Superintendent) — AFSC 3E891
- **Awarded:** With SMSgt promotion
- **Experience:** Managing EOD flight/squadron operations

### Unique Requirements — EOD
- **Joint-service qualification** — EOD badge is DoD-wide
- **EOD Badge:** Awarded after initial qualification; Senior and Master badges for experience/leadership milestones
- **Recurring proficiency training** — must maintain currency on all ordnance types; loss of currency = non-deployable
- **Pre-Positioned Equipment (PPE):** EOD teams maintain specialized UTC equipment kits
- **Secret Service support:** Selected personnel support POTUS/VIP protective details
- **High deployment tempo** — one of the most deployed AFSCs in the AF
- Does NOT typically attend Silver Flag (has own exercise/readiness events)

> ⚠️ **EOD operates semi-independently** from the rest of CE for training purposes. Their CFETP and readiness cycle have unique requirements not shared with other 3E AFSCs.

- **Equipment:** Maintain specialized CBRN detection/decontamination equipment
- **Small shops:** Typically 5–10 military per base

## CE-Specific Programs

### Prime BEEF / READINESS (AFI 10-210)

**Prime Base Engineer Emergency Force (BEEF)** is the CE deployment construct:

- **Purpose:** Organize, train, and equip CE forces for contingency and wartime operations with minimal advance notice
- **Capabilities:** Base beddown, facility construction/repair, fire emergency services, EOD, CBRN response, emergency management
- **All CE military personnel** are required to complete foundational Prime BEEF training per AFI 10-210

**Key Training Requirements (per AFI 10-210 AFGM, April 2025):**
- Silver Flag training (see below)
- Combat support training
- AFSC-specific contingency skills
- Training must be completed during **Reset or early Prepare Phase** of AFFORGEN cycle
- Training validity: **good for the duration of the AFFORGEN cycle**

### Silver Flag

- **Location:** Tyndall AFB, FL (primary); other combat support training ranges
- **Duration:** ~1 week
- **Who Attends:** All CE military personnel (except EOD, which has separate readiness exercises)
- **Purpose:** Hands-on contingency training — bare base beddown, tent city setup, runway repair, utility repair, force protection
- **Requirement:** Must complete before deployment; per AFI 10-210, should be completed during Reset/Prepare phase
- **Validity:** Good for the AFFORGEN cycle (typically 2–3 years)
- **Updated Requirements (Apr 2025 AFGM):** Revisions to training locations, crane instructor certification, and AFSC-specific specialized training

> ⚠️ **Silver Flag is deployment-gating.** Personnel who have not completed Silver Flag may be non-deployable for Prime BEEF UTCs.

### AFFORGEN Cycle & UTC Readiness

The **Air Force Force Generation (AFFORGEN)** model governs how CE forces cycle through readiness phases:

| Phase | Purpose | CE Training Focus |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **Reset** | Recovery from deployment/high ops tempo | Silver Flag, foundational training, equipment reset |
| **Prepare** | Build readiness | AFSC upgrade training, advanced courses, UTC equipment prep |
| **Ready** | Available for tasking | Maintain readiness, MICT compliance, UTC equipment validated |
| **Commit** | Deployed or committed | Execute mission |

**UTC (Unit Type Code) Readiness:**
- Unit commanders must align UTCs with AFFORGEN
- Monthly UTC planning evaluations required (AFI 10-401)
- Personnel must meet all mission training requirements before deployment (AFI 10-405, Ready Airman Training)
- Readiness reported via **Defense Readiness Reporting System (DRRS)** (classified)
- **ARIS** (Automated Readiness Information System) manages UTC resources (personnel, equipment, supplies)

### AEF Deployment Eligibility

To be deployment-eligible for AEF (Air Expeditionary Force) / AFFORGEN tasking, CE personnel must have:

1. ✅ Current Silver Flag (within AFFORGEN cycle)
2. ✅ Skill level appropriate for UTC position (typically 5-level minimum)
3. ✅ All AFSC-specific certifications current
4. ✅ Medical readiness (current PHA, dental Class 1/2, immunizations)
5. ✅ Individual equipment/mobility bag maintained
6. ✅ LOAC (Law of Armed Conflict) / SABC (Self-Aid Buddy Care) current
7. ✅ Weapons qualification current
8. ✅ CBRN training current (per EM readiness requirements)
9. ✅ Security clearance current (if required by AFSC)

### MICT (Management Internal Control Toolset) Compliance

- **Purpose:** Standardized self-inspection checklists for every CE function/shop
- **Frequency:** Continuous; inspectable at any time
- **Responsibility:** Each shop NCOIC/supervisor maintains MICT checklists
- **Content:** AFSC-specific task compliance, safety, training documentation, equipment accountability
- **Compliance required for:** Unit readiness reporting, CCIP (Commander's Inspection Program), no-notice inspections (reintroduced 2026)
- **Common CE MICT areas:**
  - Training documentation (OJT records, CDC progress, upgrade status)
  - Safety program compliance
  - Equipment/tool accountability
  - Work order management (SMS/NexGen IT)
  - Vehicle operations and maintenance
  - Environmental compliance

## Promotion-Gating Requirements Summary

The following requirements directly gate promotion eligibility or board scoring:

| Requirement | Gate Type | Applies To | Reference |
|-------------|-----------|------------|-----------|
| **CDC Completion** | Hard gate — cannot test for promotion without completing CDCs | All 3E AFSCs (5-level upgrade) | AFI 36-2502 |
| **5-Level Award** | Hard gate — must hold 5-level to test for SSgt | All 3E AFSCs | AFI 36-2502 |
| **7-Level Award** | Hard gate — must hold 7-level to test for MSgt | All 3E AFSCs | AFI 36-2502 |
| **CCAF Degree** | Soft gate — board consideration factor; not technically required but strongly weighted | All AFSCs | AF promotion board guidance |
| **AFIT WMGT 437** (Troop Construction PM) | Required for MSgt promotion (AD) | All CE 3E AFSCs (except EOD, Fire, EM — verify CFETP) | Individual CFETPs |
| **IFSAC/ProBoard Certifications** | Duty qualification — loss may affect deployability | 3E7X1 Fire Protection | DAFI 32-2001, DoDI 6055.06 |
| **NFPA 1582 Medical** | Duty qualification — loss = potential AFSC disqualification | 3E7X1 Fire Protection | DAFI 32-2001 |
| **Pesticide Applicator Cert** | Duty qualification — loss affects ability to perform primary duties | 3E4X3 Pest Management | Individual CFETP |
| **EOD Proficiency Currency** | Deployment gate — must maintain currency | 3E8X1 EOD | EOD CFETP |
| **Silver Flag** | Deployment gate — required for AEF eligibility | All CE (except EOD) | AFI 10-210 |
| **Craftsman Course** | Required for 7-level award (where applicable) | Varies by AFSC | Individual CFETPs |

### Reserve-Specific Notes (AFRC)

- AFRC personnel at 7-level still spend significant time on technical tasks (vs. AD where it shifts more toward supervision)
- Training completion timelines may differ from AD (OJT requirements adjusted for drill/AT schedule)
- Silver Flag attendance coordinated through unit deployment manager
